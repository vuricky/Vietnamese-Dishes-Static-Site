Com Tam Recipe

Ingredients for the spring rolls

  • 8 rice paper wrappers (bánh tráng)
  • 1/2 lb (225g) shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1/2 lb (225g) pork belly or shoulder
  • 3 tbsp of sugar4 oz (115g) rice vermicelli noodles, cooked
  • Lettuce leaves (butter or green leaf lettuce)
  • Thinly sliced cucumber, carrots, or bean sprouts (optional)

Ingredients for the peanut dipping sauce

  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 2 tbsp peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup water
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Crushed peanuts (for garnish)
  • Chili paste (optional for spice)

Directions

  1. Cook the shrimp and pork: Boil a pot of water and add the pork belly. Simmer for about 25-30 minutes until cooked through. Slice thinly. In the same pot, boil the shrimp until pink and cooked (about 2-3 minutes). Slice in half lengthwise.
  2. Prepare the noodles and vegetables: Cook rice vermicelli noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse with cold water to prevent sticking. Wash and prepare herbs, lettuce, and optional vegetables.
  3. Assemble the spring rolls: Fill a shallow dish with warm water. Dip one rice paper wrapper into the water, ensuring it's completely wet. Remove and lay it flat on a clean surface. Place lettuce and herbs in the lower third of the wrapper. Add a small portion of noodles, pork slices, and any additional vegetables. Lay 2-3 shrimp halves near the center of the wrapper for visibility when rolled. Fold the sides of the wrapper inward, then tightly roll it from the bottom to the top, enclosing the filling completely. Repeat for the remaining rolls.
  4. Make the dipping sauce: In a small pan, mix hoisin sauce, peanut butter, water, and sugar over medium heat. Stir until smooth and slightly thickened. Add minced garlic and simmer for 1-2 minutes. Remove from heat. Garnish with crushed peanuts and chili paste (if desired).
  5. Serve: Serve the spring rolls fresh, alongside a bowl of peanut dipping sauce.
