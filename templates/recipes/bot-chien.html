Bột Chiên Full Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1lb of rice cakes - can be found at a local Asian grocery store
  • 2 eggs for frying
  • 2-3 tbsp of vegetable oil for frying
  • 1/4 cup of green onions
  • Salt and pepper - season to desired taste

Ingredients for the sauce

  • 3 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp of vinegar
  • 1 tbsp of sugar
  • 1/4 cup of water
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1 Small chili thinly sliced

Directions

  1. Make the rice cakes: In a bowl, whisk together rice flour, tapioca starch, water, and salt until smooth. Heat a non-stick pan over medium heat and lightly grease with oil. Pour in the batter and cook, stirring occasionally, until it thickens into a sticky dough. Transfer the dough to a greased dish or container, spread it out evenly, and let it cool completely. Once set, cut the rice cake into small squares or rectangles (about 1 inch wide).
  2. Prepare the dipping sauce: In a small bowl, mix soy sauce, vinegar, sugar, and water until the sugar dissolves. Add minced garlic and sliced chili. Adjust to taste if needed.
  3. Fry the rice cakes: Heat 2-3 tbsp of oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the rice cake pieces and pan-fry until golden and crispy on both sides
  4. Add the eggs: Crack two eggs over the fried rice cakes and lightly scramble them, mixing the egg with the rice cakes as it cooks. Sprinkle chopped green onions over the top and cook until the eggs are set.
  5. Serve: Transfer the fried rice cakes and eggs to a plate. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired. Serve hot with the soy sauce dipping sauce and pickled vegetables on the side.
